##### Regression and Correlation

<Indent>

Asks "Is there a relationship between two variables?" and 
"How strong is that relationship?"

The best fitting line is the linear relationship in the data. 
<WordSpan word="Residuals" definition={wordList['residuals']}/> are the 
distance each point is from the best fit line.

The equation for linear regression is:

<Spacer/>
<Indent>
<InlineMath math={String.raw`y = a + bx`}/>
</Indent>
<Spacer/>

**_y_** is the <mark>dependent</mark> variable and **_x_** is 
the<mark>independent</mark> variable. **_a_** is the y-intercept 
and is found with the formula:

<Spacer/>
<Indent>
<InlineMath math={String.raw`a = \frac{(\sum y)(\sum x^2) - (\sum x)(\sum xy)}{n(\sum x^2) - (\sum x)^2}`}/>
</Indent>
<Spacer/>

**_b_** is the slope and is found with the formula:

<Spacer/>
<Indent>
<InlineMath math={String.raw`b = \frac{n(\sum xy) - (\sum x)(\sum y)}{n(\sum x^2) - (\sum x)^2}`}/>
</Indent>
<Spacer/>

**_n_** is the sample size (number of rows on the table). This is a lot
of work so just plug the values into a calculator like
[this one](https://www.hackmath.net/en/calculator/linear-regression).

<WordSpan word="Linear correlation coefficient" definition={wordList['linear correlation coefficient']}/> 
(r) and {' '}
<WordSpan word="Coefficient of Determination" definition={wordList['coefficient of determination']}/> 
{' '} (r<sup>2</sup>):

<Spacer/>

<Box>
<InlineMath math={String.raw`r = \frac{SS_{xy}}{\sqrt{SS_xSS_y}}`}/>

Where

<InlineMath math={String.raw`SS_x = \sum{(x-\bar x)^2}`}/><br/>

<InlineMath math={String.raw`SS_y = \sum{(y - \bar y)^2}`}/><br/>

<InlineMath math={String.raw`SS_{xy} = \sum{(x - \bar x)(y - \bar y)}`}/><br/>

<InlineMath math={String.raw`\bar x`}/> and <InlineMath math={String.raw`\bar y`}/> are the mean

</Box>

<Spacer/>

- Positive r represents a positive association (goes up), negative r represents a negative association (goes down)

- r is always between -1 and 1

- The closer to 1 or -1 the stronger it gets

- The closer to 0 the weaker it gets

- If r is 0, this means that there is no **linear** correlation
